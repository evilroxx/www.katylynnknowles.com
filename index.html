Artistic Core Values

  • I utilize collaboration, communication and organization skills during the rehearsal process as well as examine rehearsal techniques in order to determine what is effective and successful as a director
  • I research and question playwrights, scripts, and human behavior to develop a process for approaching the material and characters in an honest and inquisitive fashion
  • I use images and quotations as a major research component to communicate with actors and designers when working on productions
  • I go on a collaborative journey with my actors in a safe and honest environment by utilizing a broad range of techniques and strategies in character analysis and development in order to help the actors make strong personal connections to the characters they are portraying
  • I believe the journey and process is just as IMPORTANT as the final product

  • I provide thought provoking, constructive critique to my performers in an honest and positive fashion allowing them an opportunity to reflect, evolve and grow during the rehearsal process
  • I create a safe and positive rehearsal environment, which allows performers and designers to explore, discover, collaborate, question, and work outside their comfort zones
  • I encourage my actors to think independently, to make bold choices with fearless attitudes and to take risks
  • I lead with a clear objective and agenda to maintain an organized, sane, and focused production process where empowerment and collaboration are encouraged
  • I produce engaging pieces of theatre that will ask audience members to question, reflect, and communicate about their past, present, and future by exposing them to uncensored snapshots of life and the world in which we live

Teaching Philosophy

I am a strong advocate for providing students with an opportunity to achieve success in the performing arts by presenting students with a freedom to explore their own sense of self, their cultures, and the world in which they live. My mission is to enrich students’ lives through performance and theatre education with challenging learning activities that engage students’ minds, hearts, and bodies so that students will learn to appreciate and understand the role and influence of the performing arts on culture and society. Through their enrichment students will attain higher levels of academic achievement, gain self-esteem, and obtain higher levels of empathy and tolerance for others. This can only be achieved by providing students with a safe and creative environment where students can celebrate their differences by integrating academics, performance, and life into preparation to become responsible citizens in our 21st century pluralistic society.
